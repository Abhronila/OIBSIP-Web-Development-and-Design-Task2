Tribute to Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose

"Give me blood, and I will give you freedom."

Introduction

Subhas Chandra Bose, popularly known as Netaji, was one of the most prominent leaders of the Indian independence struggle. Born in 1897, Bose played a crucial role in mobilizing Indians against British colonial rule. He was known for his radical approach, his leadership of the Indian National Army (INA), and his alliances with foreign powers to achieve independence for India. His slogan, “Give me blood, and I will give you freedom,” remains iconic in Indian history

Biography

Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose (1897–1945*) was a prominent leader in India’s freedom struggle. Known for his fiery patriotism and the slogan “Give me blood, and I will give you freedom,” he led the Indian National Army (INA) against British rule. A visionary and revolutionary, he challenged colonialism with courage, founded the Forward Bloc, and established the Azad Hind Government. Though his reported death in 1945 remains disputed, his legacy as one of India’s greatest freedom fighters lives on.

Date of Birth:23 January 1897

Place: Cuttack, Odisha

Parents:Janakinath Bose (father), Prabhavati Devi (mother)

Occupation:Freedom Fighter, Political Leader

Life Timeline

  • 1897 –Born in Cuttack, Odisha
  • 1921 –Resigned from Indian Civil Services (ICS) to join freedom struggle
  • 1938 –Elected President of Indian National Congress (Haripura Session)
  • 1939 –Resigned from Congress, formed Forward Bloc
  • 1941 –Escaped from house arrest, fled to Germany
  • 1943 –Took command of Indian National Army (INA) in Singapore
  • 1943 –Formed Azad Hind Government (Provisional Govt of Free India)
  • 1945 –Reported plane crash in Taiwan (death controversy continues)

Achievements

  • Leadership in the Indian National Congress –Elected President in 1938 (Haripura Session), showing his popularity and leadership qualities.
  • Formation of the Forward Bloc (1939) –Created a new political wing after resigning from Congress to continue the fight for complete independence.
  • Organizing the Indian National Army (INA) –Unified and inspired thousands of Indian soldiers to fight against British rule.
  • Establishment of the Azad Hind Government (1943) –Set up a provisional government of Free India, recognized by several countries.
  • Military Campaigns in Burma and Northeast India –Led INA troops alongside Japanese forces in battles at Imphal and Kohima.
  • Promotion of Women’s Role in Freedom Struggle –Formed the Rani of Jhansi Regiment, an all-women regiment in the INA, a groundbreaking step at that time.
  • Slogans that Inspired a Nation –“Give me blood, and I will give you freedom” and “Jai Hind,” which continue to inspire generations.

Legacy

Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose remains one of India’s most celebrated freedom fighters, whose courage and vision continue to inspire generations. His creation of the Indian National Army marked a turning point in India’s independence movement. Even decades after his mysterious disappearance, Netaji lives on in the hearts of Indians as a symbol of determination, sacrifice, and patriotism. Schools, institutions, roads, and awards have been named in his honor, reminding us of his indomitable spirit and his dream of a free and strong India.

Final words of tribute

This page is a humble tribute to Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose, the fearless son of India whose life was dedicated to the cause of freedom. His ideals of courage, selflessness, and love for the motherland remain a guiding light for all of us. May we always remember his words, ‘Give me blood, and I will give you freedom,’ and carry forward his dream of a united and prosperous India.
